CS2951F ¬∑ Learning & Sequential Decision Making

The course explores automated decision making from a computer-science perspective. It examines efficient algorithms, where they exist, for single-agent and multiagent planning as well as approaches to learning near-optimal decisions from experience. Topics include MDPs, stochastic and repeated games, POMDPs, and reinforcement learning, with emphasis on generalization, exploration, and representation.

Policies

Collaboration Policy

You are free to talk about class material including homeworks with other people. However, it is forbidden for two people to submit the same text or diagrams. To ensure that you do not hand in duplicate answers, it is best if you do not look at all at each other's answers, but restrict your discussion to the ideas themselves.

Incomplete Policy

We expect everyone to complete the course on time. However, we certainly understand that there may be factors beyond your control, such as health problems and family crises, that prevent you from finishing the course on time. If you feel you cannot complete the course on time, please discuss with Prof. Littman the possibility of being given a grade of Incomplete for the course and setting a schedule for completing the course in the upcoming year. Requests for accommodations by a dean are required.

Due Dates

Projects and homeworks must be handed in by 11:59 pm on their due dates.

Late Policy

If you are ill, you may get an extension for homeworks. Please get a note from either health services or a dean and contact Prof. Littman.

Accommodations

If you feel you have physical, psychological, or learning disabilities that could affect your performance in the course, we urge you to contact SAS. We will do whatever we can to support accommodations recommended by SAS. We also intend to provide lecture capture in an attempt to make the class as inclusive and accessible as possible.

Mental Health

Being a student can be very stressful. If you feel you are under too much pressure or there are psychological issues that are keeping you from performing well at Brown, we encourage you to contact Brown's Counseling and Psychological Services (CAPS). They provide confidential counseling.

Coping with Unforeseen or Difficult Circumstances

Please feel free to talk to Prof. Littman about life events that impact your performance in the course. Additionally, Student Support Services Deans can be a helpful resource for discussing current concerns and academic and personal plans. They are available for both same-day consults and scheduled appointments.
