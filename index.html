Syllabus

Topics

  • Markov decision processes, dynamic programming
  • Planning: heuristic search, MPC, model predictive control
  • Value-based and actor-critic methods
  • Function approximation, representation learning
  • POMDPs, memory, and belief-state planning
  • Exploration and intrinsic motivation
  • Offline RL and batch-constrained learning
  • Safe RL, robustness, and evaluation
  • RL at scale: distributed training and sim2real

Policies

Collaboration

Discuss high-level ideas with peers; write code and text independently. Cite all sources and collaborators. For projects, work in teams of 2‚Äì3 unless otherwise approved.

Late Work

You have a 72-hour late budget across the term for homeworks (max 48h on any single HW). Projects follow milestone-specific rules.

Academic Integrity

We follow Brown's academic code. Use of generative tools must be disclosed and is limited to editing/ideation; generated code must be cited and validated.

Accessibility

We are committed to inclusive learning. Students with documented accommodations should contact staff and SAS early; we will arrange appropriate adjustments.
